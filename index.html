Psychospiritual Coaching

Moving from insight
into a life that is actually yours

Most of us spend years accumulating understanding about ourselves — why we do what we do, where our patterns come from, what we say we value. And still the gap remains. Between the understanding and the living. Between the person we sense we could be and the one who shows up each day.

Coaching works at the level where that gap lives — not in the past, but in the present. In the body. In the parts of you that haven't gotten the memo that change is safe.

This is not therapy. Coaching is forward-facing, goal-oriented, and time-bounded. It's most powerful when you're not in crisis but in transition — when the old identity no longer fits and you need rigorous, soulful support for what comes next.

What we work on together

  • Living in alignment with your actual values rather than inherited ones
  • Reclaiming the parts of yourself you abandoned to survive or belong
  • Shadow material that is quietly driving decisions you don't understand
  • The particular exhaustion of performing a version of yourself that doesn't fit
  • Meaning, purpose, and the question of what you are actually here to do
  • The becoming that waits on the other side of what frightens you most

About

I have always lived
in two worlds.

The one that wants evidence and the one that knows things before the evidence arrives. For a long time I tried to keep them separate. I don't anymore.

My first career was in molecular biology — the language of data, controls, and replicable results. That tension drove me out of the lab and into psychology, and then into the long project of figuring out why the standard clinical model wasn't reaching what it needed to reach.

I trained as a psychotherapist, specialized in complex and developmental trauma, and founded Bay Area Mental Health — a multidisciplinary group therapy practice I built and ran for years before selling it in 2023.

I kept looking. I trained in somatic therapy, parts work, and ketamine, MDMA, and psilocybin-assisted therapy — not because I was collecting modalities, but because I kept finding that the most important healing was happening somewhere the clinical model wasn't designed to go.

I'm not a light worker with crystals and an Instagram filter. I'm someone with graduate degrees in both molecular biology and clinical psychology who has also sat in ceremony, felt my father say goodbye the night he died, and learned to hold both of those things as real.

I am neurodivergent, and I have particular expertise working with neurodivergent clients. I write weekly in How Healing Happens on Substack. I live in the south of France with my husband, which is either a midlife crisis or a values-alignment success story, depending on who you ask.
